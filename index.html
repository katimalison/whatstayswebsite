“ ‘As I said, I have so few memories of it’ sings Kati Malison in the opening lines of What Stays, the first offering from indie rock trio Doll Spirit Vessel. If there is grief in not recalling one’s past, there is freedom in its corollary: being utterly rooted in the present. This freedom comes through in What Stays; Doll Spirit Vessel let each song become what it wants to be, whether that’s a sing-along bedroom pop anthem like “Something Small” or an odyssey through grunge, dream-pop and prog-rock (“All the Right Things…”). The effect is anything but disjointing– it serves to highlight what the songs have in common: Malison’s masterful melodies and vivid lyricism, and the exacting, acrobatic voice she wields to bring them together.”

Max, Lewis, and I made What Stays over ten days at a cabin near Crater Lake, OR. We came into the sessions without arrangements; we’d never played these songs all together and tried to use that to our advantage. We experimented with tempo, instrumentation, and even structure. “A Need” was about to be scrapped when Max called us into the living room one morning, having stayed up late and transformed it from a slog of distorted guitars to the dreamscape that opens the record.

What Stays was self-recorded in December 2020 at a cabin near Crater Lake, Oregon. It was written, engineered, and produced by me, Kati Malison. My bandmates, Lewis Brown and Max Holbrook, were collaborators in the truest sense, and contributed in far more ways than credits can convey. I wouldn’t have been able to manage the technical side of making this album without the advice of Heather Jones and Bradford Krieger. What Stays was mixed by Peter Geiser and mastered by Brandon Peralta. Kati Malison: lead vocals, backing vocals, guitar, bass, synth Lewis Brown: lead guitar, drums, edrums, synth, backing vocals Max Holbrook: bass, guitar, keys, synth, backing vocals Pepe Hidalgo: drums (tracks 6 & 10) Sophie Goldberg: backing vocals (tracks 9 & 10) Heidi Aispuro: horns (track 12) Peter Geiser: mixing, edrums (track 6) Brandon Peralta: mastering
